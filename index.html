Harmeet Kaur, PhD

Data Scientist | Bioinformatician

I am a data scientist with formal training in computational structural biology. I like taming messy data, connecting dots in them to build a complete picture. Experience encompass developing analytics pipeline, building predicitve machine learning models to communicate to relevant stakeholders through interactive dashboard. I strive for working in a cross-functional environment building robust AI powered healthcare solutions impacting human life.

In my current role at Genentech, California, I have built a React based application for exploration and discovery of Ab based therapeutics, collaborated with research scientists in analyses of structural data towards designing novel therapeutics and in preparation of training data for predictive modeling.

I worked at RCSB PDB for a short period under the supervision of Prof. John Westbrook, developing APIs of molecular structural data from various resources to build a federated resource. As a health data fellow at Insight Data Science, Boston, I developed a machine learning based web application to predict gestational diabetes in expectant women to promote timely interventions.

Prior to this, I led teams at Rutgers University, New Jersey, to extract, transform and load data retrieved through API to postgreSQL server deployed on AWS and built predictive model for risk stratification of CVD in human subjects.

One of my biggest achievements has been cracking three national fellowships (ICMR-JRF, CSIR-JRF, GATE) for pursuing doctorate. My PhD project focussed on understanding structural adaptation in antibodies of a lineage in response to different antigens.

My passion to solve real-world problem using data science took me to a Virtual DeepRacer Challenge organized by AWS in New York, where I trained a car with reinforcement learning and completed the lapse in ~18sec (2nd slot)

I am a team player and a proponent of knowledge dissemination and experiential learning to peers. For any queries and solutions, I can be reached at meet.academia@gmail.com.
