Harmeet Kaur, PhD

Data Scientist | Bioinformatician

I am a data scientist with formal training in computational structural biology with a focus on understanding the structural adaptations in antibody lineage w.r.t. to different antigens. Over the years, I have developed a keen interest in taming diverse data, connecting dots and bringing the pieces together to be presented to relevant stakeholders through visualizations and interactive dashboard.

In my current role at Genentech, California, as part of a dynamic team in Harris' lab, I have built a REACT based application for exploration and analyses of internal and external biological macromolecular data. Collaborative efforts with research scientists include analysis towards designing novel therapeutics and prepare training data for predicting antibody aggregation.

At RCSB PDB, under the supervision of Late Prof. John Westbrook, I got a chance to develop API to build a federated resource. As a health data science fellow at Insight Data Science, Boston, I developed a machine learning based web application to predict gestational diabetes in expectant women to promote timely interventions.

Prior to this, I led teams at Rutgers University, New Jersey, to extract, transform and load data retrieved through API to postgreSQL server deployed on AWS and built predictive model for risk stratification of CVD in human subjects.

Fun fact, at Virtual DeepRacer Challenge organized by AWS, New York 2020, I modified a reinforcement learning algorithm to train a car and completed the lapse in ~18sec (2nd slot). I am a team player and a proponent of knowledge dissemination and experiential learning to peers. I strive for working in a cross-functional environment building robust AI powered solutions impacting human life. If my profile aligns with your interest/requirement, feel free to reach out at meet.academia@gmail.com.
